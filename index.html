Understanding Large Language Models

How LLMs work under the hood · Feb 10, 2026
LLM architecture diagram

Large language models (LLMs) like GPT are neural networks trained on massive text datasets to predict the next token in a sequence.

They use a Transformer architecture with self-attention layers to capture long‑range relationships in text, enabling capabilities like code generation, conversation, and summarization. In this post we break down tokens, embeddings, attention heads, and why scaling up models leads to surprisingly emergent behavior.
